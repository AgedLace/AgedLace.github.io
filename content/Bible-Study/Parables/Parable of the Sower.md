## The Parable of the Sower Explained

>[!example] **Matthew 13:18**
><sup>***18***</sup> Consider, then, the parable of the sower:

- **consider**
	- [[0191 - akouó]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 191. ἀκούω (akouó) -- to hear, listen](https://biblehub.com/greek/191.htm)
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- properly, to hear (listen); (figuratively) to hear God's voice which prompts Him to birth faith within (cf. Ro 10:17). See 189 (akoē).

>[!example] **Matthew 13:19**
><sup>***19***</sup> When anyone hears the message of the kingdom but does not understand it, the evil one comes and snatches away what was sown in his heart. This is the seed sown along the path.

- **understand [it]**
	- [[G4920 - suniémi]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 4920. συνίημι (suniémi) -- to set together, fig. to understand](https://biblehub.com/greek/4920.htm)
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- (from 4862 /sýn, "together with" and hiēmi, "put, send") – properly, put together, i.e. join facts (ideas) into a comprehensive (inter-locking) whole; synthesize.
		- ("put facts together") means to arrive at a summary or final understanding (complete with life-applications). Accordingly, 4920 (syníēmi) is closely connected with discerning and doing "the preferred-will of God" (2307 /thélēma).
		- Eph 5:17: "So then do not be _foolish_ (878 _/áphrōn_), but _understand_ (4920 _/syníēmi_) what the _preferred-will_ (2307 _/thélēma_) of the Lord is (2307 _/thélēma_)."

- **snatches away**
	- [[G076 - harpazó]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 726. ἁρπάζω (harpazó) -- to seize, catch up, snatch away](https://biblehub.com/greek/726.htm)
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- properly, seize by force; snatch up, suddenly and decisively – like someone seizing bounty (spoil, a prize); **to take by an open display of force (i.e. not covertly or secretly)**.

- **at once (promptly)**
	- [[G2112 - eutheós]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 2112. εὐθέως (eutheós) -- at once, directly](https://biblehub.com/greek/2112.htm)
	- **Strong's Exhaustive Concordance**
		- as soon as, forthwith, immediately
		- Adverb from euthus; directly, i.e. At once or soon -- anon, as soon as, forthwith, immediately, shortly, straightway.

- **receives**
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 2983. λαμβάνω (lambanó) -- to take, receive](https://biblehub.com/greek/2983.htm)
	- [[G2983 - lambanó]]
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- (from the primitive root, lab-, meaning "actively lay hold of to take or receive," see NAS dictionary) – properly, to lay hold by aggressively (actively) accepting what is available (offered). 2983 /lambánō ("accept with initiative") emphasizes the volition (assertiveness) of the receiver.

>[!example] **Matthew 13:21**
><sup>***21***</sup> But since he has no root, he remains for only a season. When trouble or persecution comes because of the word, he quickly falls away.

- **root**
	- [[G4491 - rhiza]]
	- **Strong's Exhaustive Concordance**
		- root.
		- Apparently a primary word; a "root" (literally or figuratively) -- root.

- **trouble**
	- [[G2347 - thlipsis]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 2347. θλῖψις (thlipsis) -- tribulation](https://biblehub.com/greek/2347.htm)
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- properly, pressure (what constricts or rubs together), used of a narrow place that "hems someone in"; tribulation, **especially internal pressure** that causes someone to feel confined (restricted, "without options").
		- ("compression, tribulation") carries the challenge of coping with the internal pressure of a tribulation, especially when feeling there is "no way of escape" ("hemmed in").
		- By contrast, 4730 (stenoxōría) focuses on the external pressure exerted by circumstances.

- **persecution**
	- [[G1375 - diógmos]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 1375. διωγμός (diógmos) -- persecution](https://biblehub.com/greek/1375.htm)
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- (from 1377 /diṓkō, "follow, pursue") – properly, pursuit (chase); persecution – literally, "the hunt to bring someone down like an animal," trying to suppress (punish) their convictions. See 1377 (diōkō).
		- ("religious persecution") literally refers to those seeking to punish God's messengers **with a vengeance** – like a hunter trying to conquer (obliterate) someone as their "catch."
		- is used in ancient and biblical Greek for persecution (hostility) shown by confused, spiritual "leaders." For example, it applies to the Roman Emperor, Decius (ad 250-251). He killed thousands of Christians who refused to offer sacrifices in his name.]

- **he quickly falls away**
	- [[G4624 - skandalizó]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 4624. σκανδαλίζω (skandalizó) -- to put a snare (in the way), hence to cause to stumble, to give offense](https://biblehub.com/greek/4624.htm)
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- properly, set a snare ("stumbling-block"); (figuratively) "to hinder right conduct or thought; to cause to stumble" – literally, "to fall into a trap" (Abbott-Smith). See 4625 (skandalon).

- **worries**
	- [[G3308 - merimna]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 3308. μέριμνα (merimna) -- care, anxiety](https://biblehub.com/greek/3308.htm)
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- (see 3307 /merízō, "divide") – properly, a part, separated from the whole; (figuratively) worry (anxiety), dividing and fracturing a person's being into parts. See 3309 (merimnaō).

- **deceitfulness**
	- [[G0539 - apaté]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 539. ἀπάτη (apaté) -- deceit](https://biblehub.com/greek/539.htm)
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- a false impression, made to deceive or cheat – i.e. deceit motivated by guile and treachery (trickery, fraud). See 538 (apataō).

- **choke**
	- [[G4846 - sumpnigó]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 4846. συμπνίγω (sumpnigó) -- to choke](https://biblehub.com/greek/4846.htm)
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- from 4862 /sýn, "identified with" and 4155 /pnígō, "choke") – properly, choke (cut off) – **literally, "choked because joined with."**
		- ("press in on, choke") **operates in people having "wrong identifications" (aligning with sin)**. Doing this cuts a person off from Christ's provisions (His life-supply, note the prefix, syn) and leaves them inoperative (stalled, **spiritually suffocated**).
		- is **figuratively used of the deadly connections of sin, cutting off the life-support needed to live**.

- **becomes**
	- [[G1096 - ginomai]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 1096. γίνομαι (ginomai) -- to come into being, to happen, to become](https://biblehub.com/greek/1096.htm)
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- properly, to emerge, become, **transitioning from one point (realm, condition) to another**. 1096 (gínomai) fundamentally means "become" (becoming, became) so it is not an exact equivalent to the ordinary equative verb "to be" (is, was, will be) as with 1510 /eimí (1511 /eínai, 2258 /ēn).
		- means "to become, and **signifies a change of condition, state or place**" (Vine, Unger, White, NT, 109).
		- M. Vincent, "1096 (gínomai) means to come into being/manifestation **implying motion, movement, or growth**" (at 2 Pet 1:4). Thus it is used for God's actions as emerging from eternity and becoming (showing themselves) in time (physical space).

- **unfruitful**
	- [[G0175 - akarpos]]
	- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 175. ἄκαρπος (akarpos) -- unfruitful](https://biblehub.com/greek/175.htm)
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- an adjective, from 1 /A "not" and 2590 /karpós, "fruit") – properly, unfruitful.
		- ("unfruitful") **refers to any thought (action) not originated and empowered by the Lord**, i.e. not born of faith (birthed and empowered by God).
		- By definition, all decisions that are not born "of faith are sin" (Ro 14:23) – which automatically renders them eternally fruitless ("a waste"). This is the case regardless of how impressive or acceptable they are in the eyes of the world.

## Observations

- Jesus tells us to actively 'hear' or 'listen' to the explanation of the parable.
- Requirements For Good Soil
	- **Understanding** is required with the active hearing of the word.
	- If there is no understanding, the enemy comes and seizes it away.
	- Questions
		- Who is responsible for the person's understanding?
			- those who are sharing the word
			- those who are (actively) hearing the word
				- is it the act of **actively** hearing the word?
			- both??
	- **Receiving** (aggressively laying hold of; seizing) the word is also required
		- Question
		- This would lay the responsibility upon the 'hearer' ??
	- Getting '**rooted**' in the Word is also necessary
		- Again, responsibility for become rooted / grounded in the word seems to fall to the 'hearer'
		- "falling away" then is a result of not being rooted /grounded in the word ???
	- Not allowing the word to be **choked** out by worries and deceitfulness
		- NOTE - the person BECOMES unfruitful; it was not the original condition; in other words, God's Word had accomplished what it was sent out to do, but the person did not cultivate the soil of his heart and allowed distractions to choke it out, preventing any spiritual growth
	- Conclusion - "good soil" consists of ...
		- actively **hearing** the word
		- **understanding** the word so it cannot be 'stolen' or 'snatched away
		- actively **receiving** the word aggressively; seizing it and laying hold of it
		- becoming **rooted** / grounded in the word so that the world cannot kill it.
		- become whole-heartedly rooted / grounded so as to prevent becoming 'fractured' / divided by the cares of the world and/or the deceitfulness of wealth; must *actively* working at not **becoming** unfruitful
		- produce a crop - refers to the 'works' of ministry referred to in Ephesians 4:12 that we are commissioned to do so that the body of Christ will be built up.
