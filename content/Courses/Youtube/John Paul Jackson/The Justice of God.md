## Steps Needed to Obtain Justice in the World

* Recognize eligibility
* Fill out proper paperwork
* Go before a judicial panel
* Patiently waiting, knowing the settlement would be given

## Process

* You have to catch the thief
	* You don't punish the thief
	* You bring the thief before the Judge
* Judge renders the decision
* **Justice**
	* the injured receives compensation for the injustice inflicted upon them
* Need to recognize It is evil is the one that brought the iilness, injustice, etc
	* illness is an injustice on our lives; God did not bring it upon us
* ![[../../../Bible-Study/Scripture/Isaiah-30v18]]

> [!example] **Isaiah 30:18 NLT**
> 
> <sup>***18***</sup>So the LORD must wait for you to come to him so he can show you his love and compassion. For the LORD is a faithful God. Blessed are those who wait for his help.

* God is waiting for you to do something that you need to do so that He can do something justifiably on your behalf
* Then you wait for Him to take action once you bring it to the Judge
	* you pray for it, but you wait for it
* There are also an issue of "me" involved
	* am I in right-standing with God
	* am I addressing the issues God wants me to address
	* have I repented of errant thinking or wrong attitudes
	* is God justified in acting on my behalf
		* Or if He acted on my behalf, would He be endorsing my sin
	* When there are things against us due to unrepentance
		* God says "court's in recess"
		* He waits
	* When He hears "O God, I'm sorry! Please forgive me!"
		* He says "court's back in session"
		* His sentence against you is "not guilty!"
	* Sometimes we have to clean up or act before God can act on our behalf
	* 2 Corinthians 10:6 - "And we will be ready to punish every act of disobedience as soon as your obedience is completed."
	* My obedience has to be fulfilled before He acts because
		* if He acts against rebellion, He's got to judge me for my rebellion
		* If I'm clean from my rebellion, then He's free to act against rebellion without having to act against me
		* Paul teaches that we need to become "slaves of righteousness"
* You have to get close to He who is holy to become holy
* You have to get close to He who is righteous to become righteous
* Justice includes recompense for pain and injury on top of the justice due
	* 7 fold-justice
		* one to balance the scales
		* six more for pain and injury
* Justice is given to the clean

## How Do We Pray for Justice

* don't ask for long life
* don't ask God to harm the one you hurt
* don't ask God for the life of your enemy
* don't ask God to kill your `________`
* don't ask for riches
* ask for understanding to discern justice
* when you give the sentence to the person who hurt you, you just got your own justice and God will not give you justice now because you chose your justice over God's justice
* say to God, "God, I just step back, and I'm going to let you step in"
	* to the degree you step back, He steps in
	* to the degree you step in, He steps back

### How Do Your Enemy's Change?

* when you let God defend you, He changes your enemies
* Proverbs - when a man's ways please the Lord He makes even his enemies to be at peace with him.
* God can take time to execute justice on your behalf
* When you let God act on your behalf
	* The people that hurt you - their lives deteriorate
	* your life gets better, stronger, more vibrant, you have spiritual children, people looking to help you because you helped them
	* your life is full of intrigue and excitement every single day
	* their life is miserable
* Justice comes in many forms
	* Let God give it to you
* God said He would put one on your heart
	* He would give you more IF YOU NEED IT
	* but maybe this one takes care of EVERYTHING
* There are many ways God can give justice
	* we cry out for it - He gives it to us
	* we don't sentence
	* we don't suggest to the Judge what He should do
