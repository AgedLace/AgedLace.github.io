		* *HELPS Word-studies* 
		**Source** - [Strong's Greek: 3056. λόγος (logos)](https://biblehub.com/greek/3056.htm)
		* [[G3056 - lógos]] (from 3004 /légō, "speaking to a conclusion") – a word, being the expression of a thought; a saying. 3056 /lógos ("word") is preeminently used of Christ (Jn 1:1), expressing the thoughts of the Father through the Spirit.
		* [[Bible-Study/Word Studies/Greek/G3056 - lógos|G3056 - lógos]] is a common term (used 330 times in the NT) with regards to a person sharing a message (discourse, "communication-speech"). 3056 (lógos) is a broad term meaning "reasoning expressed by words."

- Verse 06
	- John (the Baptist)
	- #Bible-Study/John-The-Baptist/Sent-By-God
		- **Sent** by God - compare this to the apostles being 'sent-out' by God
		- #Bible-Study/Greek/0649-apostelló
		- *HELPS Word-studies* 
			- **Source** - [Strong's Greek: 649. ἀποστέλλω (apostelló) -- to send, send away](https://biblehub.com/greek/649.htm)
				- [[G649 - apostelló]] - (from 575 /apó, "away from" and 4724 /stéllō, "send") – properly, send away, i.e. commission; (passive) "sent on a defined mission by a superior."
				- As an intensification of 4724 /stéllō ("send"), 649 (apostéllō) focuses back to the source (the one sending), strongly connecting the sender to the one sent (His mission). This verb is used of closely connecting the Lord (the sender) to the believers He personally commissions – as with John the Baptist (Mk 11:2) and the twelve apostles (Mt 10:5; cf. also with His holy angels, Mk 13:27).
