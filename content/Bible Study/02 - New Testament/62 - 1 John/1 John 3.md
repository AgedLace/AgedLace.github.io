> [!NOTE] **1 John 3:1-3**
> 
> **Children of God**
> 
> 1 Behold what manner of love the Father has given to us, that we should be called children of God. And that is what we are! The reason the world does not know us is that it did not know Him. 2 Beloved, we are now children of God, and what we will be has not yet been revealed. We know that when Christ appears,<sup>a</sup> we will be like Him, for we will see Him as He is. 3 And everyone who has this hope in Him purifies himself, just as Christ is pure.<sup>b</sup>

> [!NOTE] **1 John 3:4-6**
> 
> 4 Everyone who practices sin practices lawlessness as well. Indeed, sin is lawlessness.<sup>c</sup> 5 But you know that Christ appeared to take away sins, and in Him there is no sin. 6 No one who remains in Him keeps on sinning. No one who continues to sin has seen Him or known Him.

- Christ appeared
	- to take away sins
		- **Thayer's** - "to cause our sins to cease, *i.e.*, that we no longer sin, while we enter into fellowship with Christ, who is free from sin, and abide in that fellowship."
- In Him
	- there is no sin

- NO ONE
	- who remains in Him
		- keeps on sinning
	- who continues to sin
		- has seen Him
		- or known Him

> [!NOTE] **1 John 3:7-8**
> 
> 7 Little children,<sup>d</sup> let no one deceive you: The one who practices righteousness is righteous, just as Christ is righteous.<sup>e</sup> 8 The one who practices sin is of the devil, because the devil has been sinning from the very start. This is why the Son of God was revealed, to destroy the works of the devil.

- #Command/Let-No-One/Deceive-You  
- #Deception    

- The one 
	- who practices righteousness
		- is righteous
		- just as Christ is righteous
	- who practices sin
		- is of the devil
		- because
			- the devil
				- has been sinning from the very start

- **THIS is why**
	- **the Son of God was revealed** 
		- *revealed - G5319 - phaneroó* #Greek/G5319-phaneroó  
			- to make visible, make clear
			- properly, illumine, make manifest (visible); 
		- **to destroy the works of the devil**
			- *destroy - G3089 - luó* #Greek/G3089-luó  
				- to loose, to release, to dissolve
				- properly, loose (unleash) let go; release **(unbind) so something no longer holds together**; (figuratively) release what has been held back ...
				- **to overthrow, do away with**
				- *COMPARE TO - Binding / Loosing* in Matthew 16 and 18

> [!NOTE] **1 John 3:11-13**
> 
> **Love One Another**\
> *(John 13:31–35; Romans 12:9–13)*
> 
> 11 This is the message you have heard from the beginning: We should love one another. 12 Do not be like Cain, who belonged to the evil one and murdered his brother. And why did Cain slay him? Because his own deeds were evil, while those of his brother were righteous. 13 So do not be surprised, brothers, if the world hates you.

- #Command/Do-Not/Be/Like-Cain/Who/Belonged-to/the-Evil-One

	- **BECAUSE** 
		- his own deeds were evil
