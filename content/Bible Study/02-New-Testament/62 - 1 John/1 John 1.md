**The Word of Life**
*(Luke 24:36–49; John 20:19–23)*

1 **That which was from the beginning**, **which we have heard**, **which we have seen with our own eyes**, **which we have gazed upon and touched with our own hands**—this is **the Word of life**. 2 And this is the life that was revealed; **we have seen it and testified to it, and we proclaim to you** the eternal life that was with the Father and was revealed to us.
#Bible-Character/John/An-Eyewitness
#Jesus/The-Word/of-Life #Jesus/The-Word/Was/In-The-Beginning

3 We proclaim to you what we have seen and heard, so that you also may have fellowship with us. And this fellowship of ours is with the Father and with His Son, Jesus Christ. 4 We write these things so that our<sup>a</sup> joy may be complete.

**Walking in the Light**
*(John 8:12–29)*

5 And this is the message we have heard from Him and announce to you: God is light, and in Him there is no darkness at all. 6 If we say we have fellowship with Him yet walk in the darkness, we lie and do not practice the truth. 7 But<sup>b</sup> if we walk in the light as He is in the light, we have fellowship with one another, and the blood of Jesus His Son cleanses us from all sin.

8 If we say we have no sin, we deceive ourselves, and the truth is not in us. 9 If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness. 10 If we say we have not sinned, we make Him out to be a liar, and His word is not in us.

**Footnotes:**

4 a BYZ and TR **your** 
7 b NA does not include **But** .

## Keywords / Word Studies

- v 1 - compare to John 1:1-2

	- the beginning - *G746 - arché* #Greek/G746-arché
		- beginning, origin
		- **HELPS Word-studies**
			- **746** _arxḗ_ – properly, from the _beginning_ (_temporal_ sense), i.e. "_the initial_ (starting) point"; (figuratively) what comes _first_ and therefore is _chief_ (foremost), i.e. has the _priority_ because ahead of the rest ("preeminent").

	- Word - *G3056 - logos* #Greek/G3056-logos
		- a word (as embodying an idea), a statement, a speech
		- **HELPS Word-studies**
			- **3056** _lógos_ (from [3004](https://biblehub.com/greek/3004.htm) _/légō_, "**speaking** to a conclusion") – a _word_, being the expression of a _thought_; **a saying**. [3056](https://biblehub.com/greek/3056.htm) _/lógos_ ("word") is preeminently used of _Christ_ (Jn 1:1), expressing the _thoughts_ of the Father through the Spirit.
			- [3056](https://biblehub.com/greek/3056.htm) (_lógos_) is a common term (used 330 times in the NT) with regards to a person sharing a message (discourse, "communication-**speech**"). [3056](https://biblehub.com/greek/3056.htm) (_lógos_) is a broad term meaning "_reasoning_ expressed by _words_."

	- of life - *G2222 - zóé* #Greek/G2222-zóé
		- life, both of physical (present) and of spiritual (particularly future) existence
		- **HELPS Word-Studies**
			- **2222** _zōḗ_ – _life_ (physical and spiritual). _All_ life ([2222](https://biblehub.com/greek/2222.htm) _/zōḗ_), throughout the universe, is _derived_ – **i.e. it always (only) comes from and is sustained by _God's self-existent life_**. The Lord _intimately shares_ His gift of life with _people_, creating each in _His image_ which gives all the capacity to know His eternal life.

- v 2 - note the repetition of the word 'revealed'

	- revealed - *G5319 - phaneroó* #Greek/G5319-phaneroó
		- to make visible, make clear, manifest, make known
		- **HELPS Word-studies**
			- **5319** _phaneróō_ (from [5457](https://biblehub.com/greek/5457.htm) _/phṓs_, "light") – properly, illumine, make _manifest_ (_visible_); (figuratively) make plain, in _open_ view; to become apparent ("graspable").
			- **[5319](https://biblehub.com/greek/5319.htm) _/phaneróō_ ("become manifest") in 1 Jn 3:2**
			- 1 Jn 3:2: "Beloved, now we are children of God, and it has not appeared as yet what we will be. We know that _if_ (Gk _ean_) He _becomes manifest_ ([5319](https://biblehub.com/greek/5319.htm) _/phaneróō_), we will be like Him, because (_hoti_) we will see Him _just as_ (Gk _kathōs_) He is."

	- proclaim - *G518 - apaggelló* #Greek/G518-apaggelló
		- to report, announce, declare
		- **HELPS Word-Studies**
			- **518** _apaggéllō_ (from [575](https://biblehub.com/greek/575.htm) _/apó_, "from" intensifying _angellō_, "announce") – properly, **to declare (report) _from_, which focuses on the _original source_ (_context_) shaping the _substance_ of what is announced**.
			- The prefix (_apo_) **distinctly "looks back" to the cause (occasion) of the announcing** which gives [518](https://biblehub.com/greek/518.htm) (_apaggéllō_) a distinct nuance-application in each case. This makes _sensitivity to the context_ essential to properly understanding the force of [518](https://biblehub.com/greek/518.htm) (_apaggéllō_) in each of its occurrences.

	- eternal - *G166 - aiónios* #Greek/G166-aiónios
		- age-long, eternal, practically eternal, unending, partaking of the character of that which lasts for an age, as contrasted with that which is brief and fleeting.
		- **HELPS Word-Studies**
			- **Cognate: 166** _aiṓnios_ (an adjective, derived from [165](https://biblehub.com/greek/165.htm) _/aiṓn_ ("an _age_, having a particular _character_ and _quality_") – properly, "_age-like_" ("like-an-_age_"), i.e. an "age-_characteristic_" (the quality describing a particular age); (figuratively) the unique _quality_ (reality) _of God's life_ at work in the believer, i.e. as the Lord _manifests His self-existent life_ (as it is in His _sinless abode of heaven_). "**Eternal ([166](https://biblehub.com/greek/166.htm) _/aiṓnios_) life operates simultaneously _outside_ of time, _inside_ of time, and beyond time – i.e. what gives time its everlasting meaning for the believer through faith, yet is also time-independent**. [See 165](https://biblehub.com/greek/165.htm) (_aiōn_).
			- [166](https://biblehub.com/greek/166.htm) **(_aiṓnios_) does not focus on the future _per se_, but rather on the _quality_ of the _age_ ([165](https://biblehub.com/greek/165.htm) _/aiṓn_) it relates to. Thus believers live in "_eternal_ ([166](https://biblehub.com/greek/166.htm) _/aiṓnios_) life" right _now_, experiencing this _quality of God's life_ now as a _present possession_**. (Note the Gk _present_ tense of _having_ eternal life in Jn 3:36, 5:24, 6:47; cf. Ro 6:23.)

## Themes

### Proclamation of Divine Revelation

- John tells us that what he, and the apostles, are proclaiming (reporting, announcing) to the world is that which they have personally ...
	- heard
	- seen with their own eyes
	- and touched with their own hands
	- they were reporting only that which they were eyewitnesses to #Bible-Character/John/An-Eyewitness  

- John uses the terms *proclaim* and *revealed* repeatedly.  

	- So, why the repetition?  
		- He is stressing that the message they are announcing is not of their own making or design, or what they have received from others. He wants it made perfectly clear they what they are proclaiming is what they have personally witnessed.  (a first-hand account)
		- v 3 tells us the reason why ...
			- ... so that we may have fellowship with them (the apostles).
			- and this fellowship is with ... #Fellowship/With/Other-Believers 
				- the Father #Fellowship/With/The-Father
				- and His Son, Jesus Christ #Fellowship/With/Jesus-Christ
		- v 4 gives us another reason ...
			- so that our joy may be complete #Joy/Made-Complete
		
	- So, exactly what did they personally witness? 
		- that God had revealed to them that Jesus is the manifestation, expression, and representation of the invisible God. 
		- that Jesus had existed with God the Father, *from the beginning*. #Jesus/The-Word/Was/In-The-Beginning
