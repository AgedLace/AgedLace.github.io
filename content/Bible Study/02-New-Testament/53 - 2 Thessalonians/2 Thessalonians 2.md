- coming - *G3952 - parousia* #Greek/G3952-parousia
	- a presence, a coming, an arrival, advent, especially of the second coming of Christ
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- **3952** _parousía_ (from _parōn_, "be present, arrive to enter into a situation") – properly, _coming_, especially the _arrival_ of the _owner_ who alone can _deal_ with a situation (cf. _LS_). [3952](https://biblehub.com/greek/3952.htm) (_parousía_) is a "technical term with reference to _the visit_ of a king or some other official, 'a royal visit' " (Souter) – "hence, in the NT, specifically of the _Advent_ or _Parousia_ of Christ" (_A-S_).
		- [3952](https://biblehub.com/greek/3952.htm) (_parousía_) is "used in the east as a technical expression for the _royal visit of a king, or emperor._ The word means literally 'the being beside,' thus, 'the personal presence' " (K. Wuest, 3, _Bypaths_, 33).

- annihilate - *G2673 - katargeó* #Greek/G2673-katargeó
	- to render inoperative, abolish
	- make idle (inactive), make of no effect, annul, bring to naught
	- discharge, server, separate from
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- **2673** _katargéō_ (from [2596](https://biblehub.com/greek/2596.htm) _/katá_, "_down_ to a point," intensifying [691](https://biblehub.com/greek/691.htm) _/argéō_, "inactive, idle") – properly, idle _down_, _rendering something inert_ ("_completely_ inoperative"); i.e. being of _no effect_ (_totally_ without force, completely brought _down_); done away with, cause to _cease_ and therefore abolish; make invalid, abrogate (bring to nought); "to make idle or inactive" (so also in Euripides, _Phoen_., 753, _Abbott-Smith_).
		- "[2673](https://biblehub.com/greek/2673.htm) (_katargéō_) means 'to make completely inoperative' or 'to put out of use,' according to _TDNT_ (1.453)" (J. Rodman Williams, _Renewal Theology_ "God, the World & Redemption," 389).

- majesty - *G2015 - epiphaneia* #Greek/G2015-epiphaneia
	- appearance, manifestation, glorious display
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- **2015** _epipháneia_ (from [1909](https://biblehub.com/greek/1909.htm) _/epí_, "_on_, fitting," which intensifies [5316](https://biblehub.com/greek/5316.htm) _/phaínō_, "show forth, appear") – properly, a _fitting manifestation_ (literally, "an epiphany").
		- [2015](https://biblehub.com/greek/2015.htm) (_epipháneia_) is the root of the English term, "epiphany."
		- 2015 (_epipháneia_) literally suggests an appearing that builds on (Gk _epi_) on the characteristics of a particular situation. Accordingly, [2015](https://biblehub.com/greek/2015.htm) _/epipháneia_ ("_conspicuous_ appearing") emphasizes the _fitting impact_ Christ's _visible_ appearance will have on the _entire_ world – i.e. all who see it (saved and unsaved). In the NT, [2015](https://biblehub.com/greek/2015.htm) (_epipháneia_) occurs six times – **always used of _Christ's coming_** (once of His _first_ coming, 2 Tim 1:10).

- judgment will come upon - *G2919 - krinó* #Greek/G2919-krinó
	- judge, whether in a law-court or privately: sometimes with the cognate nouns emphasizing the notion of the verb,
	- decide, think (it) good
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- **2919** _krínō_ – **properly, to separate (distinguish), i.e. judge; come to a _choice_ (decision, judgment) by making a _judgment_ – either positive (a verdict in favor of) or negative (which rejects or condemns).**
		- J. Thayer comments that "the proper meaning of [2919](https://biblehub.com/greek/2919.htm) (_krínō_) is _to pick out_ (choose) _by separating_" (as also used in Homer, Herodotus, Aeschyl., Xenophon, Plato). [2919](https://biblehub.com/greek/2919.htm) _/krínō_ ("distinguish, judge") typically refers to making a determination of right or wrong (innocence or guilt), especially on an _official_ (_legal_) _standard_. We only _judge_ ([2919](https://biblehub.com/greek/2919.htm) _/krínō_) accurately by _intelligent comparison and contrast_ based on God's word, i.e. to approve (prefer) what is correct and reject what is inferior (wrong).
		- [2919](https://biblehub.com/greek/2919.htm) (_krínō_) is used of "bringing to trial" (the trying of fact) in a court of law.
		- 2919 (_krinō_) originally meant "_separate_." So Homer, of Ceres separating the grain from the chaff (Iliad, v, 501). Thence, 'to distinguish, to pick out, to be of opinion, to judge' " (_WS_, 418).
