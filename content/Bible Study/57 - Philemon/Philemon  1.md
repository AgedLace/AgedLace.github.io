## Philemon 1

#NT/Philemon-1

**Greetings from Paul and Timothy**  
#Bible-Character/Paul #Bible-Character/Timothy  
*(Philippians 1:1–2; Colossians 1:1–2)*

1 Paul, *a prisoner of Christ Jesus*, and Timothy our brother,  
#Bible-Character/Paul/A-Prisoner-of-Christ-Jesus 

To Philemon *our beloved fellow worker*, 2 to Apphia our sister, to Archippus *our fellow soldier*, and to the church that meets at your<sup>a</sup> house:  
#Bible-Character/Philemon/Beloved-Fellow-Worker #Bible-Character/Apphia #Bible-Character/Archippus/A-Fellow-Soldier   
#Question/What-Is/Difference-Between/Worker-and-Soldier  

3 Grace and peace to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

**Philemon's Faith and Love**

4 I always thank my God, remembering you in my prayers, 5 because I hear about your faith in the Lord Jesus and your love for all the saints. 6 I pray that your *partnership in the faith* **may become effective** *as you fully acknowledge **every** good thing that is ours in Christ*. 7 I take great joy and encouragement in your love, because you, brother, have refreshed the hearts of the saints.  
#Faith/Partnership-In/Effective #Faith/Partnership-In/Effective/Contingent-Upon/Acknowledging/Every-Good-Thing-That-Is-Ours-In-Christ #In-Christ/Acknowledge/Every-Good-Thing/That-Is-Ours #Command/Acknowledge/Every-Good-Thing/That-Is-Ours-In-Christ  

**Paul's Appeal for Onesimus**  
#Bible-Character/Onesimus  

8 So although in Christ I am bold enough to order you to do what is proper, 9 I prefer to appeal on the basis of love. For I, Paul, am now aged, and a prisoner of Christ Jesus as well.

10 I appeal to you for my child Onesimus,<sup>b</sup> whose father I became while I was in chains. 11 *Formerly he was useless* to you, but *now he has become useful* both to you and to me. 12 I am sending back to you him who is my very heart.  
#Bible-Character/Onesimus/Pauls-Spiritual-Child #Bible-Character/Onesimus/Means-Useful   

13 I would have liked to keep him with me, so that on your behalf he could minister to me in my chains for the gospel. 14 But *I did not want to do anything without your consent*, so that your goodness will not be out of compulsion, but by your own free will. 15 For perhaps this is why he was separated from you for a while, so that you might have him back for good— 16 no longer as a slave, but better than a slave, as a beloved brother. He is especially beloved to me, but even more so to you, both in person and in the Lord.

17 So *if you consider me a partner*, receive him as you would receive me. 18 But if he has wronged you in any way or owes you anything, charge it to my account. 19 I, Paul, write this with my own hand. I will repay it—not to mention that you owe me your very self.

20 Yes, brother, *let me have some benefit<sup>c</sup>* from you in the Lord. Refresh my heart in Christ.

21 Confident of your obedience, I write to you, *knowing* that you will do even more than I ask.

22 In the meantime, prepare a guest room for me, because I hope that through your prayers I will be restored to you.

**Additional Greetings**

23 Epaphras, my fellow prisoner in Christ Jesus, sends you greetings, 24 as do Mark, Aristarchus, Demas, and Luke, my fellow workers.  
#Bible-Character/Epaphras/Fellow-Prison-in-Christ-Jesus #Bible-Character/Mark #Bible-Character/Aristarchus #Bible-Character/Demas #Bible-Character/Luke     
25 The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ be with your spirit.<sup>d</sup>

**Footnotes:**

2 **a** *You*  and *Your*  are singular throughout this letter, except in verses 3, 22, and 25.  
10 **b** *Onesimus*  means *useful*  (see verse 11) or *beneficial*  (see verse 20).  
20 **c** Greek *onaimēn* , a play on the name *Onesimus*  
25 **d** BYZ and TR include *Amen*.

### Keywords / Word Studies

- *partnership - G2842 - koinónia* #Greek/G2842-koinónia 
	- From *koinonos*; partnership, *i.e.* participation, or intercourse, or benefaction.
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- 2842 *koinōnía* (a feminine noun) – properly, what is *shared* in *common* as the basis of *fellowship* (*partnership, community*).
- *effective - G1756 - energés* #Greek/G1756-energés
	- Effective, productive of due result, work. From *en* and *ergon*; **active, operative** (same word(s) used in Hebrews 4:12 AMPC, *i.e.* 'active', 'operative', 'energizing', and 'effective')
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- Cognate: 1756 *energḗs* (an adjective) – properly, *energized*, full-of-energy (operative). See 1754 (*energeō*).
- *fully acknowledge - G1922 - epignósis* #Greek/G1922-epignósis
	- From *epiginosko*; recognition, *i.e.* full discernment, acknowledgement
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- Cognate: 1922 *epígnōsis* (from 1909 /*epí*, "*on*, fitting" which intensifies 1108 /*gnṓsis*, "knowledge gained through first-hand relationship") – properly, "contact-knowledge" that is appropriate ("apt, fitting") to first-hand, experiential knowing. This is defined by the individual context. See 1921 (*epignōskō*).

### Theme

Paul is sending Onesimus, who used to be Philemon's slave, back to him. Paul asks Philemon to release Onesimus and return him so that he can assist Paul in ministering the Gospel.
