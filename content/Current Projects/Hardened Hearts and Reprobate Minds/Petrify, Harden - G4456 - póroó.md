## Greek

- *Harden G4456 - póroó* #Greek/G4456-póroó  
	- **Strong's Concordance**
		- to petrify, to harden, render callous, petrify
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- **4456** _pōróō_ (from _pōros_, a kind of marble) – properly, made of _stone_; (figuratively) _insensible_; dull, _unperceptive_ as _a rock_; calloused (hardened); i.e. unresponsive (dense), completely lacking sensitivity or spiritual perception.
		- From the time of Hippocrates, [4456](https://biblehub.com/greek/4456.htm) (_pōróō_) means "_harden_," like a _formed callus_ which _petrifies_ (cf. _DNTT_, 2, 153).
		- 4456 (_pōróō_) was applied to _bony formations on the joints_ (a "_callus_," _ossification_) – hence meaning "to petrify" (as in "covering over with a callus"). [4456](https://biblehub.com/greek/4456.htm) (_pōróō_) is _only used figuratively_ in the NT meaning "to deaden (dull), to make (render) obtuse (dull, dead)" (Souter).
