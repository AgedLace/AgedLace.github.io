- *rebellion - G646 - apostasia* #Greek/G646-apostasia  
	- **Strong's Concordance**
		- defection, revolt, apostasy
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- **646** _apostasía_ (from [868](https://biblehub.com/greek/868.htm) _/aphístēmi_, "leave, depart," which is derived from [575](https://biblehub.com/greek/575.htm) _/apó_, "away from" and [2476](https://biblehub.com/greek/2476.htm) _/histémi_, "stand") – properly, departure (implying _desertion_); _apostasy_ – literally, "a leaving, from a previous _standing_."
	- **NAS Exhaustive Concordance**
		- **NASB Translation**
			- apostasy (1), forsake (1).
	- **Strong's Exhaustive Concordance**
		- falling away, apostasy
		- Feminine of the same as *apostasion*; defection from truth (properly, the state) ("apostasy") -- falling away, forsake.

#Question/Apostasia/Does-the-Greek/Invalidate/OSAS    

If one is leaving / departing / forsaking from a previous standing, then were these not professing Christians at one time? Would this not refute the *once saved always saved* doctrine? #False-Doctrine/Once-Saved-Always-Saved
