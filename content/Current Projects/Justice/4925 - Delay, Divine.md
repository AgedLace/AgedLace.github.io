Delays by God do not result from any failure on his part, but reflect unrealistic human expectations concerning his actions. God delays his vengeance in order to give time for human repentance.

God delays to allow for repentance

To give time for repentance

[Luke 13:6-9](https://biblehub.com/luke/13-6.htm); [Romans 2:4](https://biblehub.com/romans/2-4.htm); [2 Peter 3:8-9](https://biblehub.com/2_peter/3-8.htm) _See also_ [Matthew 3:7-11](https://biblehub.com/matthew/3-7.htm) pp [Luke 3:7-10](https://biblehub.com/luke/3-7.htm); [1 Timothy 2:4](https://biblehub.com/1_timothy/2-4.htm); [James 5:7](https://biblehub.com/james/5-7.htm); [Revelation 2:21](https://biblehub.com/revelation/2-21.htm)

In response to repentance

[1 Kings 21:29](https://biblehub.com/1_kings/21-29.htm) _See also_ [1 Kings 21:21-28](https://biblehub.com/1_kings/21-21.htm)

Where there is no repentance, God does not delay

[Ezekiel 12:25](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/12-25.htm) _See also_ [Ezekiel 21:24-26](https://biblehub.com/ezekiel/21-24.htm); [Revelation 2:16](https://biblehub.com/revelation/2-16.htm)

God delays judgment for the sake of his name

[Isaiah 48:9](https://biblehub.com/isaiah/48-9.htm) _See also_ [Nehemiah 9:30-31](https://biblehub.com/nehemiah/9-30.htm)

Jesus Christ delayed visiting Lazarus for God's glory

[John 11:1-6](https://biblehub.com/john/11-1.htm) _See also_ [John 11:11-23](https://biblehub.com/john/11-11.htm)-44

The giving of the Holy Spirit was delayed until Pentecost

[Luke 24:49](https://biblehub.com/luke/24-49.htm) _See also_ [Acts 1:4](https://biblehub.com/acts/1-4.htm); [Acts 2:1-4](https://biblehub.com/acts/2-1.htm)

God acts when he wants and nothing can delay him

[Hebrews 10:37](https://biblehub.com/hebrews/10-37.htm) Referring to the second coming of Jesus Christ. _See also_ [Habakkuk 2:3](https://biblehub.com/habakkuk/2-3.htm) Referring to the LORD's delay in sending the Babylonians to conquer Judah and take her inhabitants into exile; [Revelation 22:20](https://biblehub.com/revelation/22-20.htm)
