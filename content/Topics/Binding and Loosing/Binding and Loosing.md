#NT/Matthew-16v18-19  

> [!NOTE] **Matthew 16:18-19**
> 
> 18 And I tell you that you are Peter, and on this rock I will build My church, and the gates of Hades will not prevail against it. 19 I will give you the keys of the kingdom of heaven. Whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will be loosed in heaven."

## Verse 18

- *"on this rock"* - the 'rock' of Peter's confession that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of the living God.

- *Peter - G4074 - Petros* #Greek/G4074-Petros  
	- **Strong's Concordance**
		- "a stone" or "boulder"
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- **4074** _Pétros_ (a _masculine_ noun) – properly, a stone (pebble), such as a small rock found along a pathway. [4074](https://biblehub.com/greek/4074.htm) _/Pétros_ ("small stone") then **stands in contrast to [4073](https://biblehub.com/greek/4073.htm) _/pétra_ ("cliff, boulder,"** _Abbott-Smith_).
	- "[4074](https://biblehub.com/greek/4074.htm) **(_Pétros_) is an isolated rock and [4073](https://biblehub.com/greek/4073.htm) (_pétra_) is a cliff"** (_TDNT_, 3, 100). "[4074](https://biblehub.com/greek/4074.htm) (_Pétros_) always means a _stone_ . . . such as a man may throw, . . . versus [4073](https://biblehub.com/greek/4073.htm) (_pétra_), a _projecting rock_, _cliff_" (S. Zodhiates, _Dict_).

- *rock - G4073 - petra* #Greek/G4073-petra  
	- **Strong's Concordance**
		- a (large mass of) rock
		- rock, ledge, cliff, cave, stony ground
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- **4073** _pétra_ (a feminine noun) – "a _mass of connected rock_," which **is distinct from [4074](https://biblehub.com/greek/4074.htm) (_Pétros_)** which is "a _detached_ stone or boulder" (_A-S_). [4073](https://biblehub.com/greek/4073.htm) (_pétra_) is **a "solid or native rock, rising up through the earth**" (Souter) – a _huge mass of rock_ (a _boulder_), such as a _projecting cliff_.
		- 4073 (_petra_) is "a projecting _rock_, _cliff_ (_feminine_ noun) . . . 4074 (_petros_, the _masculine_ form) however is a _stone_ . . . such as a man might throw" (S. Zodhiates, _Dict_).

- This is the same word in Jesus' parable of a house built on a rock. (Matthew 7:24-25; Luke 6:48)
- Is also used in the phrase *"rock of offense* (Romans 9:33; 1 Peter 2:8) which refers to Jesus; thus Jesus' statement that according to Peter's confession that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of the living God, He is the *rock* upon which He will build His church.
- Paul confirms this in 1 Corinthians 10:3-4 -
	- They all ate the same spiritual food and drank the same spiritual drink; for they drank from the spiritual rock that accompanied them, **and that rock was Christ.**
- Also, compare this to Nebuchadnezzar's dream and the *stone cut out, but not by human hands,* and which *became a great mountain and filled the whole earth.* in Daniel 2:34-35. And it's interpretation in Daniel 2:44-45, *the God of heaven will set up a kingdom that will never be destroyed, ... will itself stand forever.*

- *gates - G4439 - pulé* #Greek/G4439-pulé  
	- **Strong's Concordance**
		- a gate
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- **4439** _pýlē_ (a feminine noun) – a _large_ door; an _entrance-gate_ to a city or fortress; a _door-gate_. [4439](https://biblehub.com/greek/4439.htm) _/pýlē_ ("a _door-gate_") typically refers to the _exit_ people _go out_, i.e. **focusing on what _proceeds out of it_.**
		- **"Gates" in antiquity generally represent authority/power**.

- *Hades - G86 - hadés* #Greek/G86-hadés  
	- **Strong's Concordance**
		- Hades, the abode of departed spirits
	- **HELPS Word-studies**
		- **86** _hádēs_ (from [1](https://biblehub.com/greek/1.htm) _/A_ "not" and _idein/eidō_, "see") – properly, the "unseen place," referring to the (invisible) realm in which all the dead reside, i.e. the present dwelling place of all the departed (deceased); _Hades_.

- *will not prevail against - G2729 - katischuó* #Greek/G2729-katischuó  
	- **Strong's Concordance**
		- to overpower
		- prevail against, overpower, get the upper hand
	- only used 3 times in the New Testament
		- Matthew 16:18
		- Luke 21:36
		- Luke 23:23 

- So, Jesus is teaching that the demonic authorities / powers coming out of the "gates of hell" shall NOT prevail against the Church He builds.  
- He is the *rock* prophesied in Nebuchadnezzer's dream. This *rock* strikes the feet of the statue representing the last nations ruling the world at the end times. It crushes and shatters these so thoroughly that they become like chaff on the threshing floor while He, and His church, become a great mountain and fills the whole earth. (Daniel 2:34-35)

	- **Matthew Henry's Concise Commentary**
		- 16:13-20 Peter, for himself and his brethren, said that they were assured of our Lord's being the promised Messiah, the Son of the living God. **This showed that they believed Jesus to be more than man.** Our Lord declared Peter to be blessed, as the teaching of God made him differ from his unbelieving countrymen. Christ added that he had named him Peter, in allusion to his stability or firmness in professing the truth. The word translated rock, is not the same word as Peter, but is of a similar meaning. **Nothing can be more wrong than to suppose that Christ meant the person of Peter was the rock. Without doubt Christ himself is the Rock, the tried foundation of the church; and woe to him that attempts to lay any other! Peter's confession is this rock as to doctrine.** If Jesus be not the Christ, those that own him are not of the church, but deceivers and deceived. Our Lord next declared the authority with which Peter would be invested. He spoke in the name of his brethren, and this related to them as well as to him. They had no certain knowledge of the characters of men, and were liable to mistakes and sins in their own conduct; but they were kept from error in stating the way of acceptance and salvation, the rule of obedience, the believer's character and experience, and the final doom of unbelievers and hypocrites. In such matters their decision was right, and it was confirmed in heaven. But all pretensions of any man, either to absolve or retain men's sins, are blasphemous and absurd. None can forgive sins but God only. And **this binding and loosing, in the common language of the Jews, signified to forbid and to allow, or to teach what is lawful or unlawful.**
	- **Barnes' Notes on the Bible**
		- ... Whatsoever thou shalt bind … - The phrase "to bind" and "to loose" was often used by the Jews. It meant to prohibit and to permit. To bind a thing was to forbid it; to loose it, to allow it to be done. Thus, they said about gathering wood on the Sabbath day, "The school of Shammei binds it" - i. e., forbids it; "the school of Hillel looses it" - i. e., allows it. When Jesus gave this power to the apostles, he meant that whatsoever they forbade in the church should have divine authority; whatever they permitted, or commanded, should also have divine authority - that is, should be bound or loosed in heaven, or meet the approbation of God. They were to be guided infallibly in the organization of the church:
		- 1. by the teaching of Christ, and,
		- 2. by the teaching of the Holy Spirit.
		- **This does not refer to persons, but to things - "whatsoever," not whosoever. It refers to rites and ceremonies in the church.** Such of the Jewish customs as they should forbid were to be forbidden, and such as they thought proper to permit were to be allowed. Such rites as they should appoint in the church were to have the force of divine authority. Accordingly, they commanded the Gentile converts to "abstain from pollutions of idols, and from fornication, and from things strangled, and from blood" [Acts 15:20](https://biblehub.com/acts/15-20.htm); and, in general, they organized the church, and directed what was to be observed and what was to be avoided. **The rules laid down by them in the Acts of the Apostles and in the Epistles, in connection with the teachings of the Saviour as recorded in the evangelists, constitute the only law binding on Christians in regard to the order of the church, and the rites and ceremonies to be observed in it.**
